Graph Modeling

From tactical behavior analytics (T-UEBA) to spatial assembly modeling (Augrade)

My graph modeling work converges on one boundary question: what should stay explicit in structure, and what should be absorbed by learned representation? The same tension appears in cyber behavior modeling and 3D assembly inference.

T-UEBA: temporal heterogeneity first

In tactical security, graphs encode users, hosts, processes, events, and cross-source relations over time. The point is not graph aesthetics; it is preserving causal legibility while enabling adaptive inference under shift.

  • Temporal graph updates over streaming events
  • Multi-plane risk scoring and confidence calibration
  • Operator-facing drilldown from score to evidence chain

Augrade: geometry and editability

For 2D→3D BIM/CAD transformation, graph-only representations are insufficient. Assemblies also contain reusable, program-like structure. The stronger hypothesis is a graph-program hybrid with deterministic validators outside the learned core.

  • Geometry-native perception and stable object tokenization
  • Typed relation graphs for support, adjacency, and dependency
  • Program layer for motifs, hierarchy, and allowed edit grammars

Shared design law

Use learned models for compression and proposal; use explicit systems for invariants and hard constraints. This split preserves both capability growth and operator trust.
